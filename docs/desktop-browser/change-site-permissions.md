# How do I change site permissions?

Control what content a website can show you and which data they can access by default.

## Change settings for all sites

1. On your computer, open Herond.
2. At the top right, click `Menu` > `Settings`.
3. Under `Privacy and Security`, click `Site and Shields Settings`.
4. Click the permission you want to update.

## Permissions that can be changed

- **Shield Status:** Sites that can block trackers, ads, and fingerprinting using Shields.
- **Location:** By default, Herond asks you if a site can see your exact location information.
- **Camera:** Some sites can ask you to use your camera and microphone.
- **Microphone:** Some sites can ask you to use your camera and microphone.
- **Notifications:** Some websites can show notifications on your computer desktop. By default, Herond asks you if a site can show notifications.
- **Cookies and site data:** Third-party cookies options.
- **JavaScript:** JavaScript helps make sites more interactive and rich in content.
- **Images:** Images are allowed by default.
- **Popups and redirects:** By default, pop-ups and redirects are blocked from showing up automatically.
- **Sound:** By default, sites will allow media content to play with sound — use this permission to allow or block specific websites from doing so.
- **Zoom levels:** You can set how much you zoom in on certain websites.
- **PDF documents:** By default, Herond opens PDFs in the Herond PDF viewer. To download PDFs instead of opening them, turn on *Download PDF files instead of automatically opening them in Herond*.
- **Protected content IDs:** Choose to let Herond play protected content.
- **Insecure content:** Insecure content is blocked by default on secure sites.

## Change settings for a specific site

You can allow or block permissions for a specific site. The site will use its settings instead of the default settings.

1. On your computer, open Herond.
2. Go to a website.
3. To the left of the web address, click the icon you see: **Secure**, **Not secure**, or **Dangerous**.
4. Click **Site and Shields settings**.
5. Change a permission setting. Your changes will automatically save.
