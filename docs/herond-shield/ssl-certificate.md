# What does SSL Certificate do?

First, let's explore the fundamental role of SSL certificates. In the realm of online security, SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) certificates play a pivotal role in safeguarding your sensitive data and ensuring secure communication between your device and websites. By encrypting the data exchanged, SSL certificates protect against unauthorized access, maintaining the confidentiality and integrity of your online interactions.

![SSL Certificate](/img/herond-shield/what-does-ssl-certificate-do--file-1eHdnydZVW.png)

In Herond Browser, we prioritize your privacy and security. That's why we integrate SSL certificate support -- we have the option to upgrade your connection to HTTPS where only sites with SSL certificates can be let through, empowering you to browse the web with confidence and peace of mind. As you navigate through websites, Herond Browser seamlessly handles SSL certificates, verifying their authenticity and establishing encrypted connections for secure data transmission.
